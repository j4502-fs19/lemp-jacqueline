A midwife crisis: the only African-American midwife in Missouri

Brittany Kellman explains how she began her organization, Jamaa Birth Village, in Ferguson, Mo., Monday, Nov. 4, 2019. “I started Jamaa out of my home, which is here in Ferguson, about two minutes away from here. And in January 2016, we began prenatal care, providing midwifery care right here in the community.”

Kellman holds a fetal doppler (left) and pinard horn (right), both of which are used to listen to a fetal heartbeat, in Ferguson, Mo., Monday, Nov. 4, 2019. “So the more and more I centered my life on holistic wellness, the more and more my passion came out. And I was like wait, I actually want to serve women and prevent this from happening… I can become a midwife.”

Kellman describes how she started Jamaa Birthing Village, Ferguson, Mo., Monday, Nov. 4, 2019. “I got a vision to create a birthing village for us, by us, where women, whether they decided to have a baby or not have a baby, so even if they were just menstruating women, they could have a safe, comfortable place to come to be seen.”
